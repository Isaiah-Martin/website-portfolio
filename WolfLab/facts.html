The grey wolf is a keystone species. A keystone species is a species that has a disproportionately large effect on its environment relative to its abundance. They play a key role in maintaining the structure of an ecological community. An example of a keystone species is a predator that prevents herbivores from eliminating dominant plant species. A lack of wolves was negatively affecting the Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem. The elk population increased since there was a lack of predators so this led to overgrazing. Also, the coyote population increased since there was a lack of competition for resources. This led to a decrease in the population of foxes and hawks since the population of small game had declined. This chain of events occurred because wolves were not present to keep the ecosystem in balance.


Fun Facts!

  1. Wolves live in packs of up to 20.
  2. A single wolf can be up to 5ft in length, standing at 3ft.
  3. One of the heaviest wolves on record weighed 130lbs.
  4. Wolves generally prefer larger prey such as deer, but will settle for something smaller, such as rabbit.
  5. Wolves are estimated to live up to 13 years old.
  6. The strongest male and female pairing lead the pack and are known as alphas.
  7. Pack alphas are the only couple to mate.
  8. The entire pack is expected to assist in childbearing.
  9. A wolf's howl is a method of communication that is the loudest when in harmony with others. A single wolf howl can be heard up to 10km.
  10. Their long muzzle ends with a hyper sensitive nose, which is one hundred times better than a human's.
  11. Their thick fur helps them survive the bitter climates.
  12. Wolves hunt in packs by stalking and attacking old, sick or weak animals.
  13. A wolf can eat up to 9kg on one meal alone. This includes the bones, fur and meat.
  14. A single wolf can travel up to 200 km in 24 hours.
